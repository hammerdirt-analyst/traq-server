Section: Site Factors

The site factors are the elements in the surrounding environment that contribute to the health of the tree. This includes a history of the trees health, the topography, the built environment, any recent changes or construction or installations around the tree, the conditions of the soil the tree lives in, the prevailing winds, the overall weather patterns and the slope of the land as well as the aspect (direction the slope is facing). In summary:

- History of failures
- Topography (flat/slope, slope %, aspect)
- Landscape environment
- Site changes (none, grade change, site clearing, changed soil hydrology, root cuts, describe)
- Soil conditions (limited volume, saturated, shallow, compacted, pavement over roots, %, describe)
- Prevailing wind direction
- Common weather (strong winds, ice, snow, heavy rain, describe)
